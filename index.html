The hardness rating, measured on the Mohs scale, indicates a gemstone's resistance to scratching and its suitability for different jewelry applications. Stones with higher hardness ratings (7 and above) are generally better for rings and bracelets that experience frequent contact and potential impact. Softer stones require more careful handling and are best reserved for earrings, pendants, or jewelry worn on special occasions rather than daily.
